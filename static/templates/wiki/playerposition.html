This section will investigate whether a classifier can be trained to predict a soccer players' position on field by only using their Wikipedia page.

The massive Wikipedia corpus of the soccer players' pages has been tokenized, stemmed and narrowed down to a bag-of-words, which was used to train the classifier. Additionally, a training set of the corpus labelled with the correct positions was used to further train the classifier.

The classifier's predictions of 10 random soccer players' field positions are presented in the table below.

As it is evidence in the result, the classifier correctly predicts all the soccer players' field positions. However, when the accuracy of the classifier is calculated, it revealed that it only classified 31% (+/- 1%) of the input correctly.

It is worth noting that the real position vs. the predicted position might only be partially correct, e.g. as it is evidence for Abdulaziz Hatem and Tomasz Kupisz. For Kupisz both Midfielder and Forward are predicted, whereas his real position is only Midfield. This would result in an incorrect classification.
