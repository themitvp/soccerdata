Friendship Paradox

In this section it will be investigated whether the well known network friendship paradox occur in the soccer player network. Furthermore, is the average market value for a soccer player's linked connections higher than his own market value?

Firstly, the classic friendship paradox was investigated. In this investigation, it was checked if a soccer player is connected with other soccer players who in average are connected with more soccer players.

Secondly, the market value was used as a parameter in the friendship paradow to find out if soccer players typically play with other players of higher market value.

The table below shows the output:

Type True False % True % False
Degree 10583 4090 72,1% 27,9%
Market Value 8990 5683 61,3% 38,7%

The results show that there is a tendency for the friendship paradox to be true for both the degree and the market value.
