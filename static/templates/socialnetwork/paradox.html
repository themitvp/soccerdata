Friendship Paradox

In this section, it will be investigated if the well-known network friendship paradox occur in the soccer player network. Furthermore, is the average market value for a soccer player's linked connections higher than his own market value?

For the classic friendship paradox, it was examined if a soccer player is connected with other soccer players who in average have a higher number of connections.

For the next investigation, the market value was then used as a parameter in the friendship paradox. This will show if soccer players in general play with other players of higher market value.

The table below shows the two outputs from the investigation:

Type True False % True % False
Degree 10583 4090 72,1% 27,9%
Market Value 8990 5683 61,3% 38,7%

72,1% of the soccer players are linked with other soccer players with a higher amount of links.

61,3% of the soccer players have a lower market value than their linked soccer players.

The result shows that there is a tendency for the phenomenon to be true for both the degree and the market value.
