Old Players Are More Loyal

This section concerns the hypothesis that old soccer players are more loyal than young soccer players.

Firstly, the soccer players are plotted in a histogram showing the distribution of the number of players vs. age.

It shows that most players are in the twenties, but there are still a sufficient amount of players in the thirties to find a tendency among them.

The mean number of transfers in each age group vs. the age were plotted as seen in the graph below. The graph reveals a relatively linear growth up to around the age of 35. From that point, the players have a tendency to have played in fewer clubs. This is interesting, as older players have had the chance to play in several clubs, but at that age the graph decreases.

The hypothesis that older players are more loyal than young players cannot be confirmed or rejected. However, the investigation shows a tendency that older players are in fact playing in fewer clubs hence they might be more loyal.
